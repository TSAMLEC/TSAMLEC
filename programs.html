MLEC Programs

Cambridge Academy

The Cambridge Academy is part of the University of International Examinations (CIE), the world’s leading provider of academic and technical qualifications internationally. Designed to develop highly successful students globally, the CIE also features the Advanced International Certification of Education (AICE) which provides a pre-college/university curriculum that emphasizes the importance of broad study for those who are eligible. Our school, Miami Lakes Educational Center, has the Cambridge Academy available with an AICE curriculum.


Engineering

Purpose is to give students the foundation of knowledge and technological experiences oriented in the application of engineering, how it affects our lives and the importance of choosing our future careers. The program focuses on work-related skills and stresses the understanding and demonstration of technical machines, instruments, materials, processes, and systems in the engineering business/industry. Students are able to earn multiple certifications that include:
• Certified Solidworks Associate - (CSWA)
• Certified SolidWorks Professional - (CSWP)
• Certified Solidworks Expert - (CSWE)
• Mastercam Associate certification
• Pre-rec Engineering cerification


Information Technology Academy

Companies, schools and most homes have computers, networks, web sites and electronic devices that rely on technology to process and use information. The Information Technology Academy is an exciting learning community that provides students with technical knowledge and skills for employment in computer technology by offering three Career Pathway choices:
• Computer Systems Technology (Cisco Networking)
• Electronic Technology
• Web Design Technology
This is the perfect academy for students who love computers, electronic gadgets, working with their hands, and anything to do with technology.


Electronic Technology

Electronics is the core building block for every area of technology today. Electronic Technology trains students for employment as service, assembly, and/or installation technicians in the electronics industry. Students gain general knowledge in all electronic areas, including Avionics, Digital Technician, and Communication Technician. Students assemble electronic circuits, use soldering techniques, design and construct amplifier and digital/microprocessor circuits, and build radios.


IT Network Administrator (Cisco)

Since computers and networks impact our lives daily, careers in computer networking are among the highest in demand and salary today. The Cisco Networking Academy is an exciting industry-supported program leading to certification in CCNA (Certified Cisco Network Associate) and successful careers in Computer Technologies, Network Administration, and Network Engineering. The program teaches computer hardware and software, network design/deployment/ management, with state-of-the-art real-world equipment such as routers, switches and servers used to connect the world.


Web Design

Most companies market their products and services by establishing web sites that are exciting, eye catching and user friendly. Web Development provides the technical knowledge and skills for employment in Web Site Design and Management. Students learn application and developer tools such as the Adobe Photoshop, Flash, Dreamweaver, and WordPress. Creatively plan, develop, and publish well-designed web sites combining effective navigation with graphics, text, color, and multimedia. Our Web Design students design sites for teachers and industry.


Clubs

Technology Student Association (TSA), VEX Robotics and Battle Bots, and Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers (SHPE) are all hosted by the leader of the Cambridge Engineering section, Paul Kynerd. Our work area contains a lab area located at the front of the classroom armed with computers in a 3-row setup. The back is the hands-on area where students get to work and demonstrate the physical aspects of the engineering process.
